Verified Deep Learning

A book by Aws Albarghouthi

Deep learning has transformed the way we think of software and what it can do. But deep neural networks are fragile and their behaviors are often surprising. In many settings, we need to provide formal guarantees on the safety, security, correctness, or robustness of neural networks. This in-progress book covers foundational ideas from formal verification and their application to reasoning about deep learning.

The book is comprised of 3 parts. A full PDF is coming soon.

Part I Neural networks and correctness

The first part defines neural networks as data-flow graphs, a formulation that will serve as our basis for the rest of the book. Additionally, we will survey a number of correctness properties that are desirable of neural networks and place them in a formal framework.

Part II Constraint-based verification

The second part discusses constraint-based techniques for verification. As the name suggests, we construct a system of constraints and solve it to prove (or disprove) that a neural network satisfies some properties of interest.

Part III Abstraction-based verification

The third part discusses abstraction-based techniques for verification. Instead of executing a neural network on a single input, we can actually execute it on an infinite set and show that all of those inputs satisfy desirable correctness properties.

Parts 2 and 3 are disjoint; the reader may go directly from Part 1 to Part 3 without losing context.
